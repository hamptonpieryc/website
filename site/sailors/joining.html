Thinking about joining?

We welcome dinghy sailors from complete beginners to expert sailors and our sailing calendar offers a mix of racing, training, crusing and general sailing. Our main activity is dinghy racing, but the Club also serves as a useful base for those who just prefer sailing their boats in a safe, controlled environment. For new and inexperienced sailors there is a free training programme.

If you are interested in joining, we recommend just coming down to the club on our open days or anytime we are sailing. There is no need to book, but if you let us know in advance we can make sure there is club member to greet you.

Please note that we only sail in mono-hulled dingies. There are other clubs locally that sail multi-hulled boats.

New to sailing

Never been in a dinghy? We run a free training programme over the season to take complete beginners to confident sailors. We start the training on our club boats, and if you don't have your own dinghy these can rented for a small fee as you progress. Our training is run by RYA approved trainers assisted by experienced sailors and is based around the RYA Level 2 course.

All you need to get started is clothing to wear in the boat. A wetsuit is ideal, but if the weather is ok a splash proof top is fine. All sailors must wear a buoyancy aid - don't worry if you dont have your own as there are club ones to borrow.

Once you know the basics sailing a dingy, one of the best ways to learn on your own is to go out racing. Watching the way more experienced sailor set their sails, tack and gybing is a great for picking up skills This might sound a little bit scary, so we run races like the confectionary cup especially for novices, and the Fleet Captains are also there to help.

Improving sailors

We offer a great environment for the improving sailor with a good mix of general sailing and racing. Our training programme will help you improve and if new to racing the Fleet Captains can help you out.

Experienced sailors

For experienced sailors we have a full racing calendar over the full season. We race in slow, medium and fast fleets, with a mix of boats in each fleet, handicapped using the Portsmouth Yardstick.
