Mini Discover Sailing Day
June 2023

The forcast is looking great, so HPYC is running a mini "Discover Sailing" day on Saturday24th June. Please note that as this being run as part of our regular training sessions, we will only be on the water from 3PM until between 4 and 5PM. For feel just to turn up on the day, but if you could let us know beforehand that would be great as it will help us plan. Coronation Party
April 2023

Please come along to have some fun and to support the Club as we celebrate the new King's coronation on Bank Holiday Monday, 8 May, starting at 14:00. This is open to all, not just Club members, so please also feel free to circulate the attached poster as widely as you may wish to include family and/or friends - the more the merrier! There is no charge for attendance.

It's entirely optional but everyone is invited to wear red, white and blue and there will be a competition for the best crown/hat with a royal theme, with separate prizes for adults and children.

Fun activities we are planning include "Pin the crown on the King", "Royal Who am I?", etc, and, as long as the weather lets us remain outside, Boules (Petanque) and maybe even a Tug of War!

Food will, as recorded on the poster, be kept simple with freshly prepared Hot Dogs, Hamburgers, Pizzas and Ice Cream. Vegetarian options will, of course, also be available. And please don't forget we have a well-stocked bar with prices that are amongst the best in town.

There is no need to make firm commitments in advance. But, to help gauge numbers for catering, if you are interested in joining in it would be greatly appreciated if you would send a short message to the social team at socialhpyc@gmail.com.

HPYC awarded £500 from the Opportunities Fund
March 2023

The Club was recently awarded two grants from the "Opportunities Fund" for activities that improve the safety and wellbeing of the local community. £200 was donated towards the cost of a replacement battery for the defibrillator and £300 towards the new safety boat trailer. We would like to pass on our thanks to Grace Paget and Councillor Peter Vickery-Jones for their support of the Club.
