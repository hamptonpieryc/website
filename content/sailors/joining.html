Thinking about joining?

We welcome dinghy sailors from complete beginners to expert sailors and our sailing calendar offers a mix of racing, training, cruising and general sailing. Our main activity is dinghy racing, but the Club also serves as a useful base for those who just prefer sailing their boats in a safe, controlled environment. For new and inexperienced sailors there is a free training programme.

If you are interested in joining, we recommend coming to the Club on our open days or anytime we are sailing. There is no need to book, but if you let us know in advance we can make sure there is club member to greet you.

Please note that we only sail in mono-hulled dinghies. There are other clubs locally that sail multi-hulled boats.
New to sailing

Never been in a dinghy? We run a training programme over the season to develop complete beginners to confident sailors. We start with training using our club boats, and if you don't have your own dinghy these can rented for a small fee as you progress. Our training is run by RYA approved trainers assisted by experienced sailors and is based around the RYA Level 2 course.

All you need to get started is appropriate clothing to wear in the boat. A wetsuit is ideal, but if the weather is warmer a splash proof top and shorts is fine. All sailors must wear a buoyancy aid. Don't worry if you don't have your own as there are plenty of club ones to borrow.

Once you know the basics of sailing a dinghy, one of the best ways to learn on your own is to try racing. Watching the way more experienced sailors set their sails, tack and gybe is a great way to pick up skills. This might sound a little scary, so we run races like the confectionary cup especially for novices, and the Fleet Captains are also there to help.
